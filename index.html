The itinerary

13 days, city by city.

6 countries, 8 cities, zero guesswork. Every day is planned so you get the real version of Europe not the rushed, tick-the-box version.

Day 01
Arrive in Amsterdam

Land at Schiphol, check in, and let Amsterdam start doing its thing. Canal reflections, houseboats, the smell of stroopwafels still warm from the cart. The city begins working on you the moment you step outside. Meet the group, get settled, and take in your first proper European evening. The camera comes out before you even reach the hotel. Obviously.

Arrival Hotel check in Group meetup
Day 02
Amsterdam city tour and Zaanse Schans

Dam Square, the Anne Frank House exterior, the Royal Palace. Amsterdam's most iconic spots with a guide who actually knows the stories behind them. Then out to Zaanse Schans where real windmills still turn, a working cheese farm sits roadside, and wooden clogs are being made in real time in front of you. Back to Amsterdam for a canal cruise at dusk. The kind of day that fills an entire camera roll before dinner happens.

Zaanse Schans Canal cruise Dutch culture
Day 03
Amsterdam to Paris

Travel to Paris. There is a specific moment you step out of the hotel and the light is somehow different, the architecture somehow better, the streets more photogenic than any city you have walked before. That moment hits everyone, every single time. Evening free to wander, eat, and take it all in slowly. Your first Paris night. Nothing needs to be planned for it to be perfect.

Paris arrival Hotel check in Free evening
Day 04
Paris highlights

Place Vendome, Opera Garnier, Champs Elysees, Arc de Triomphe. The full guided Paris edit in one day. Then up to the second floor of the Eiffel Tower for views that genuinely justify the whole trip. Followed by a Seine cruise at golden hour. If you are going to form opinions about Paris, today is when they happen. They will be very strong, very good opinions.

Eiffel Tower Seine cruise City tour
Day 05
Paris day two

More Paris. The Louvre, Montmartre, the little streets behind the streets, patisserie windows that deserve their own dedicated reel. Three nights here means you actually get to know the city rather than just skim it. Today is the day you find your favourite corner, your favourite cafe, your one view that makes you quietly decide you want to come back. Everyone finds something different.

Louvre Montmartre Free time
Day 06
Paris to Germany

Travel to Germany. Half-timbered houses, cobblestone streets, a riverfront glowing at golden hour. The kind of place that looks exactly like a period film and turns out to be just a regular Tuesday afternoon. Germany is consistently underestimated on European itineraries. After today, it will not be underestimated by anyone in this group.

Germany Old town Hotel check in
Day 07
Germany to Switzerland, Mt. Titlis

Into Switzerland, then straight up to Mt. Titlis. Snow, in the middle of summer. The revolving cable car, a glacier cave at 3,020 metres, views that stop you mid-sentence. Nobody is too cool for Mt. Titlis. Everyone up there is just standing still, taking the same photo from ten different angles, and not feeling the slightest bit embarrassed about it.

Mt. Titlis Snow experience Cable car
Day 08
Switzerland sightseeing, Lucerne

Lucerne's old town, the Chapel Bridge over glassy water, Lake Lucerne opening up ahead of you. Switzerland simply decides to be unreasonably beautiful and just keeps going. The backdrop here makes everything you wear look good. Full day to explore, shoot, and eat Swiss chocolate that costs more than it probably should but is completely worth every franc.

Lucerne Chapel Bridge Lake views
Day 09
Switzerland to Innsbruck

Travel to Innsbruck, Austria. A city with the Alps as its literal backdrop in every single direction you look. The Golden Roof, the Baroque old town, the Maria Theresien Strasse laid out beautifully ahead of you. The kind of city that genuinely surprises you because it was not on your radar and then quietly becomes one of your favourites. Happens every time with this stop.

Innsbruck Golden Roof Alpine backdrop
Day 10
Innsbruck to Padova

Cross into Italy and arrive in Padova, home to one of Europe's oldest universities, arcaded walkways designed for slow walks and great outfits, and piazzas where the whole city seems to come out together in the evening. The gelato situation improves immediately at the Italian border. This is not an opinion. It is a fact confirmed by every traveller who has made this exact crossing.

Padova Italy arrival Piazzas
Day 11
Padova to Tuscany

Through the Italian countryside to Tuscany. Rolling hills, rows of cypress trees, vineyards and olive groves doing exactly what they do in every Italian film you have ever loved. The light turns golden in the afternoon and the whole landscape looks like someone applied a perfect filter. Tuscany is the filter. Florence is nearby. Everything smells like warm bread and good olive oil.

Tuscany Countryside Florence nearby
Day 12
Tuscany to Rome

Travel to Rome. The Colosseum, the Trevi Fountain, the Vatican. Everything you have kept on your mood board for years, now right in front of you at full scale. Nothing prepares you for how large the Colosseum actually is. Or how loud the crowd around the Trevi Fountain gets. Or how good Roman pasta tastes at 10pm in a side street. Final group dinner in the Eternal City. A dinner that makes the whole trip.

Rome Colosseum Trevi Fountain
Day 13
Fly home from Rome

Last breakfast in Rome. Check out. Airport. You board the flight home with more content than you know what to do with, a notes app overflowing with cafe names, and a quiet but firm argument forming in your head for why 13 days was not quite enough. It never is. That is the whole point.

Departure Last breakfast
